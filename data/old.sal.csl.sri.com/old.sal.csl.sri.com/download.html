SAL Version 2.2 (released 23 Apr 2004)

SAL is free for noncommercial research purposes, but requires registration and acceptance of its licensing terms.

SAL 2.2 includes SALenv 2.2 (symbolic, witness, bounded, and infinite-bounded model checkers, and related tools), and ICS 2.0b.

  • Binary Distributions. This is what most users will need. Distributions are available for Linux, Solaris, Mac OS X, and Cygwin. After clicking the link, you will be able to download, but to activate your copy of SAL you will need to register by filling in a simple online form that will automatically generate a license certificate and email it to you.
  • Source Distribution. This link is password protected. The source distribution requires the SRI FM license to be printed, signed, and returned to SRI for processing. You will then receive a login and password.
For commercial evaluation or use, and other licensing questions, please contact fm-licensing@csl.sri.com.

Older Versions

SAL 2.1 (released 24 Jan 2004)

SAL 2.1 includes SALenv 2.1 (symbolic, witness, bounded, and infinite-bounded model checkers, and related tools), and ICS 2.0.

SAL 2.0 (released 12 Nov 2003)

SAL 2.0 includes SAL2XML 2.0 (parser), SALenv 2.0 (symbolic, bounded, and infinite-bounded model checkers, and related tools), and ICS 2.0. Note that unlike SAL 2.1, this version of SAL2XML requires Java.

SAL 1.0 (released June 2002)

Note that this requires the same license as the source distribution described above.

SAL 1.0 provides an explicit-state model checker and related tools. It uses a slightly older version of the SAL language (see the sal.dtd file in the tar file). Explicit-state model checking is very useful in certain circumstances and SAL users who require this capability currently must use SAL 1.0. We do plan to port the SAL explicit-state model checker to SAL 2.X in the future. SAL 1.0 and SAL 2.0 can coexist.
